Oil stocks extend rally to two sessions

By Lisa Sanders

Published: Sept 1, 2004 4:50 p.m. ET

Share

By

LisaSanders

DALLAS (CBS.MW) -- Oil stocks closed higher after the American Petroleum Institute and the Energy Department reported significant declines in crude supplies.

The API said crude inventories for the week ended Aug. 27 fell by 8.1 million barrels, and the Energy Department reported a drop of 4.2 million barrels. See Futures Movers.

The Philadelphia Oil Service Index OSX, -5.06% was up 2.3 percent to close at 112.35, the highest level in a month. The Amex Oil Index XOI, -4.58% rose 1.1 percent to close at 646.65, and the Amex Natural Gas Index XNG, -4.02% added 1.4 percent to close at 249.55.

The October crude contract jumped $1.88, or 4.5 percent, to close at $44 a barrel, reversing a recent pullback. October natural gas shed 10.9 cents to settle at $4.965 per million British thermal units.

"Over the last month or so, natural gas prices have been moderating since there has been little cooling demand, more liquefied natural gas imports at the front half of the summer and no real Gulf of Mexico hurricane downtime or infrastructure loss of consequence," Merrill Lynch analyst John Herrlin wrote in a research note.

Merrill expects full natural gas storage of 3.1 trillion to 3.2 trillion cubic feet by the start of the withdrawal season, but doesn't expect prices to fall much below $5 per thousand cubic feet due to high crude prices.

"But 2005 natural gas prices could be down 10 percent vs. 2004 realizations without strong winter weather demand, given our current assumptions on the U.S. economy," Herrlin added.

He recommended investors put their money in U.S. companies Kerr-McGee KMG, -4.98% and Apache APA, -5.67% as well as Canadian stocks Canadian Natural Resources CNQ, -3.17% and Talisman Energy TLM, +0.00% if they want "volume growth and 'balanced or oil production leverage' in the United States."
